## Thoughts

A beautiful reminder that intensity can come from pacing as much as plot. Dostoevsky captures the ache of loneliness and the warmth of human connection in just 100 pages.

## What Struck Me

- The **dreamer's inner world** feels vivid and relatable
- **Nastenka's letter** at the end—devastating in its honesty
- How **fantasy becomes a prison** when reality disappoints

## Favorite Passage

"Your hand is cold, mine burns like fire. How blind you are, Nastenka!"
